## Tim's raw writing (verbatim)

> (POST 3: I like but maybe needs an extra bunch of thanks to splice.com. Good title)
>
> I remembered those mario paint videos and thought… everyone will want to do this for their ships. The night I got that tool working was one of the saddest of development and almost killed the game. I had heard and imagined amazing synthwave music in my head, and what I just made was going to put work on players to make utterly discordant garbage that they were forced to hear on repeat. I briefly thought "Maybe I need to be a musician first" but put that aside because it was too far a leap from what my life currently was.
>
> Yes, I did look into AI music generators. But even those, when you have sounds in your head that excite you and then you try to tell AI to generate them, it's just… bad. It wasn't practical and it wasn't good and artists would probably hate me for it anyway. Then I found splice and still, my reaction was "I wish this site didn't suck so bad," but basically that was on me, because I was reading it's capabilities all wrong. Luckily, and I do mean super lucky I bothered, I downloaded their mobile app and discovered the stacks feature (screenshot). It was a total dream. Loops, or things I didn't know were called DAWs [un-abbreviate here] could be set to a unified BPM, and set to a key.
>
> The dream was back alive and I moved so fast to pick, upload, and implement this beautiful music that I now find myself imagining conversations with people where they say "Why did you choose 120BPM and the key of D Sharp Minor?" to which instead of having an answer of creative intention, I'll just have to say "idk I was like crazy excited".
>
> I have dreams of making in-game environments that trigger key changes and beat changes (call them what they actually are), but I also know I tend to get carried away adding new features instead of shipping a solid base of them. Gotta stay focused with this one.
